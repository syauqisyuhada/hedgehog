History of Hedgehog

A hedgehog is any of the spiny mammals of the subfamily Erinaceinae, in the eulipotyphlan family Erinaceidae. There are seventeen species of hedgehog in five genera found through parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and in New Zealand by introduction. There are no hedgehogs native to Australia and no living species native to the Americas (the extinct genus Amphechinus was once present in North America). Hedgehogs share distant ancestry with shrews (family Soricidae), with gymnures possibly being the intermediate link, and they have changed little over the last 15 million years. Like many of the first mammals, they have adapted to a nocturnal way of life. Their spiny protection resembles that of the unrelated porcupines, which are rodents, and echidnas, a type of monotreme.

The name hedgehog came into use around the year 1450, derived from the Middle English heyghoge, from heyg, hegge ("hedge"), because it frequents hedgerows, and hoge, hogge ("hog"), from its piglike snout. Other names include urchin, hedgepig and furze-pig.

How to raise a mini Hedgehog

If we raise pets, of course we will take care of them properly. Like bathing or cleaning the body and also not forgetting to feed.

How to bathe a Hedgehog

Mini hedgehogs also need a bath so they don't smell and their health is maintained. How to bathe it is arguably quite easy, you only need to prepare a small bucket of water, a toothbrush, and baby soap or shampoo. Put the mini hedgehog in a bucket, then pour baby shampoo over its body and brush gently. The parts that really need to be cleaned are the stomach and between the body. Rinse the body thoroughly and make sure that no shampoo remains. After rinsing, dry the body with a clean cloth or towel.

How to feed a Hedgehog

Paying attention to nutritional intake properly is the easiest way to maintain the health of the mini hedgehog. In the wild the mini hedgehog likes to eat insects, worms, caterpillars, millipedes, mealworms, snails, small animals, and various fruits. You can give the mini hedgehog the food that has been mentioned. Besides that, you can also give him mini hedgehog food which can be purchased at pet stores. Mini hedgehogs can also be given dry food specifically for dogs or cats, but make sure they are small. Don't give foods that contain lactose.
